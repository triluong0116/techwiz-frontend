HOW TO CHOOSE LENSES
Eyeglass frames are not only a fashion accessory but also protect your health. Therefore, it is often difficult for you to choose the right frame, especially when buying online. With 10 years of experience in the field of prescription glasses, Hibou will help you choose frames that are both beautiful and suitable for your face, style and usage needs.
When you have refractive errors (nearsightedness, astigmatism, farsightedness), the frames become “part of your body” – an inseparable accessory. The right frame should not only look good, but also fit and be comfortable. To choose a beautiful, standard eyeglass frame, you need to do the following 4 steps:

1. Determine the right eyeglass shape

2. Determine the frame size

3. Determine frame color

4. Determine frame material

STEP 1: CHOOSE THE GLASS SIZE THAT FITS YOUR FACE SIZE

Put your hair back, look in the mirror and determine what your face shape is like: round or angular, short or long… Usually, your face will fall into these four common face types. Each face will suit different eye shapes:

1.1. Eyeglass frames suitable for round faces

The round face is a popular face shape with soft, rounded lines. If you have a round face, you should choose rectangular, square or polygonal frames.

1.2. Eyeglass frames suitable for oval and oval faces

An oval face is considered an ideal face shape. To keep this harmony, choose glasses that are equal to or more than the width of your face, oval lenses will look best.

1.3. Eyeglass frames suitable for angled, square faces

Square faces need frames that soften the edges. Oval or circular eyeglasses will balance out and add lightness to the edges of a square face.

1.4. Eyeglass frames suitable for heart faces, high cheekbones

The heart-shaped face has a balance between the width of the forehead and the narrowness of the chin. Eyeglasses with a narrow top and a wide bottom border will give your face more depth and balance. Round or square eyeglasses with curved edges will help draw attention away from a high and wide forehead.

1.5. Eyeglass frames suitable for long, round faces

A pear-shaped face with long, rounded features will need wide angled frames. You will fit rectangular, square or hexagonal frames with a relatively long vertical.

If you still feel that your face is not really close to the above illustrations, simply choose the opposite shape of your glasses to your face. For example, a face with a lot of round, soft lines will work well with square, angular glasses. A face with pronounced cheekbones and jawline will be more harmonious with round or rounded glasses.

STEP 2: CHOOSE THE FRAME SIZE THAT FITS YOUR FACE

Like clothes, frames also come in different sizes. However, glasses frames in the Vietnamese market usually only have one size in models. Frames that are too big or too small are not only unsightly, but they can also cause eye problems: easily misaligned, stunned, or accelerated. You need to determine the right frame size for you to save time when choosing frames.

2.1. How to determine your frame size

- For those of you who have or are wearing glasses, please measure the size of the frame you are using that is most suitable. You can measure in 2 ways:

Method 1: Read the size of the glasses on the frame of the glasses

Method 2: Measure the horizontal and vertical dimensions of the lenses

- If you have never worn glasses, measure your face size. Take a ruler and place it horizontally under your eyes to determine the right lens width. Then you apply the average size chart for Hibou's frames below.

2.2. Standard frame sizes for each face

Large face should wear glasses with size: 140 - 145 mm wide; Vertical from 55 mm.

The average face should wear glasses with the size: 133 - 137 mm in width; Vertical from 43 mm.

If you have a large face, you should wear glasses with the following dimensions: 125 - 130 mm in width; Vertical from 30 mm.

STEP 3: CHOOSE THE COLORS OF YOUR SKIN AND HAIR

If you choose the shape of the frame, you apply the CONTRACT principle (Choose the shape of the eye that is opposite to the shape of the face), then when choosing the color, you apply the principle of IMPLICATION. Whatever your skin tone is, choose the same shade of glasses.

If your skin has a cold undertone: red, pink or blue, choose black, silver, sepia, gray-green, plum red, pink or blue frame colors.

This also applies to hair color, you can choose frames that match your hair color. Those with light hair dye (bleached hair) should wear prominent colors such as silver, blue or transparent frames.

STEP 4: CHOOSE THE FRAME MATERIAL TO FIT YOUR NEEDS

Material is also an important factor when choosing a frame. Most people can choose according to their liking. But for some of you with sensitive skin, you should limit the choice of alloy frames because it can cause skin irritation. Instead, you choose flexible plastic frames or titanium frames will be more suitable.

Given the number of heights, you should opt for frames made of titanium or cellulose acetate elastomeric molded plastic frames. These materials have high durability that will increase the time of use as well as create comfort when wearing for you.

Applying the above 4 steps, you can easily choose a beautiful and standard frame that fits your style and needs. If you are still confused, send a message via facebook/messenger so that Hibou can advise you on the most suitable glasses frame even when you are far away!
